The Mindset

Khan has explicitly stated that he believes "the human body is made to struggle." Rather than seeking comfort as he ages, he deliberately puts his body and mind under pressure.

The Philosophy

"The human body is made to struggle"

This is the core of everything. Comfort is not the goal. The body and mind are designed to face resistance, adapt to it, and grow stronger. Avoiding hardship is not living — it is decaying. Every challenge is a signal to your system: improve.

Mental Fitness

Daily mental discipline

He maintains a strict mental routine, which currently includes reading the Quran for an hour daily, alongside reading newspapers and books. This practice prevents cognitive decline and keeps the mind sharp, focused, and grounded.

Mental fitness is not optional. Just as the body needs exercise, the mind needs daily discipline. Reading, reflecting, and learning are the exercises of the brain.

Daily Routine

The anchor of every day

Routine is the container of discipline. Khan wakes up early, maintains a rigid daily schedule, and goes to sleep early. The structure itself is the foundation — without it, nothing else holds.

Mental Exercises

5 exercises for mental toughness

Practical practices to build the same mental resilience Khan relies on.

01

Daily reading

Read for at least 30 minutes every day. Sacred text, philosophy, or history. Not social media. Not news. Deep reading.

02

Embrace discomfort

Do one thing every day that is uncomfortable. Cold shower. Early wake-up. A difficult conversation. Train your comfort zone to expand.

03

Morning stillness

Spend the first 10 minutes of your day in silence. No phone, no input. Let your mind settle before the world rushes in.

04

Reframe failure

When something goes wrong, ask: "What is this teaching me?" Khan says failure only destroys you if you let it demoralize you.

05

Gratitude reflection

End each day naming three things you're grateful for. Not possessions — struggles, lessons, people. This builds perspective.
