The Fasting

In a 2022 interview, Khan revealed that one of his biggest health secrets is regular fasting. The science behind it is called autophagy.

When you fast, every organ of your body gets a message that it's not getting sufficient food, and hence every organ works hard to perform its function… when organs are brought into a state of stress, they function better than before.
— Imran Khan
The Science

What is autophagy?

Autophagy is the body's natural cellular cleansing process. During periods of fasting, when the body is not receiving food, cells begin to break down and recycle damaged components. Old, dysfunctional proteins and cellular debris are cleared out, and new, healthy cells are regenerated in their place.

This process is linked to reduced inflammation, improved metabolic health, better brain function, and slowed aging. It is the biological mechanism behind the ancient practice of fasting — and Khan has applied it with precision for decades.

The Protocol

The 16:8 method

The most practical and sustainable fasting protocol is 16:8 — fast for 16 hours, eat within an 8-hour window. This aligns naturally with the light-dinner-heavy-lunch structure.

How to practice 16:8

  • Finish dinner by 7:00 PM
  • Skip breakfast the next morning (coffee/tea without milk is fine)
  • Eat your first meal at 11:00 AM (lunch)
  • Eat your last meal by 7:00 PM (light dinner)
  • Drink water, black coffee, or green tea during the fast
The Timeline

What happens hour by hour

Your body shifts through distinct metabolic states during a fast.

12h
Ketosis begins

Glycogen stores deplete. Body shifts to burning fat for energy. Mental clarity improves.

16h
Autophagy starts

Cellular cleanup begins. Damaged proteins are broken down and recycled. Inflammation drops.

24h
Deep autophagy

Immune cell regeneration accelerates. Growth hormone increases. Stem cell activity rises.

48h
Peak regeneration

Maximum cellular cleanup. Old immune cells replaced. Brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) surges.

The Philosophy

Stress makes you stronger

Khan's approach to fasting reflects his broader life philosophy: the human body thrives under controlled stress. Rather than avoiding discomfort, he deliberately introduces it — giving his organs a reason to work harder, adapt, and grow more resilient.

This is the same principle behind exercise, cold exposure, and mental challenges. The body is not a machine that wears out with use — it is a system that strengthens in response to demand.

Common Mistakes

What to avoid

⚠
Breaking the fast with a heavy meal — Break gently with something light (dates, fruit). A heavy meal after fasting shocks the digestive system.
⚠
Not drinking enough water — Dehydration mimics hunger. Drink water throughout the fasting window.
⚠
Fasting erratically — Consistency matters more than duration. A regular 16:8 schedule trains your body's adaptation.
⚠
Eating poor quality food in the eating window — Fasting doesn't cancel out junk food. Whole foods only.
