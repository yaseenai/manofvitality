The Diet

Clean. Natural. Disciplined. No steroids, no supplements — just real food, strategically timed.

☕

Morning

Coffee, porridge, and dates. A clean start — slow-release energy, fiber, and natural sugars to fuel the day ahead.

🍗

Lunch Main Meal

Heavy proteins — chicken or mutton — balanced with lentils (daal) and fresh salads. Dense nutrition for recovery and strength.

🍎

Dinner

Extremely light. Only fruit, milk, and dates. No heavy meals before sleep — giving the digestive system full rest overnight.

🌱

Superfoods

Honey, chia seeds, walnuts, and green tea. Natural energy boosters packed into the daily routine for antioxidants and essential fats.

Sample Day

A full day of eating

Every meal has a purpose. Here is exactly how a day looks, from sunrise to sleep.

6:00 AM

☕ Coffee + Dates

Black coffee to wake the system. 2-3 dates for quick natural energy and fiber to start digestion gently.

7:00 AM

🍝 Porridge

Oats or grain porridge with milk. Slow-release carbohydrates that sustain energy for hours without spiking blood sugar.

1:00 PM

🍗 Chicken/Mutton + Daal + Salad

The heaviest meal of the day. Dense protein for muscle repair, lentils for complex carbs and fiber, fresh salad for micronutrients and enzymes.

4:00 PM

🌱 Green Tea + Walnuts + Chia

Afternoon superfoods. Green tea provides antioxidants and gentle caffeine. Walnuts deliver omega-3s. Chia seeds offer fiber and protein.

7:00 PM

🍎 Fruit + Milk + Dates

Light dinner. The digestive system rests overnight while the body repairs. Fruit provides vitamins, milk offers tryptophan for sleep, dates for gentle sweetness.

The Science

Why this timing works

Heavy lunch, light dinner — Your body's digestive enzymes and metabolic rate follow a circadian rhythm. They peak in the middle of the day when your body expects food. Eating your largest meal at lunch aligns with this natural cycle, improving digestion and nutrient absorption.

Light dinner — Eating lightly in the evening gives your digestive system 12-14 hours of rest before the next morning's meal. This rest period is essential for autophagy (cellular repair) to occur effectively overnight.

No processed food — Whole foods require more energy to digest, keep you fuller longer, and provide a complete nutrient profile that isolated supplements cannot replicate.

The Rules

Food: Do's & Don'ts

Simple principles that govern every meal.

✓ Do Eat
  • ✓ High-protein breakfast — porridge, dates, coffee
  • ✓ Heavy, protein-rich lunch — chicken, mutton, daal
  • ✓ Fresh salads with every main meal
  • ✓ Light dinners — only fruit, milk, dates
  • ✓ Superfoods daily — honey, chia, walnuts, green tea
  • ✓ Natural, whole foods — locally sourced
✗ Don't Eat
  • ✗ No steroids or artificial supplements
  • ✗ No heavy dinners — digestive system needs rest
  • ✗ No processed or junk food
  • ✗ No overeating — portion control at every meal
  • ✗ No artificial sugars or energy drinks
  • ✗ No late-night eating
