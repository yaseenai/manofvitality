The Exercise

As a former elite athlete, Khan treats physical training as a mandatory part of every single day — no excuses, even in confinement.

Even with limited space in Adiala Jail, his daily routine includes physical exercise using an exercise bike, a barbell, and 9-kilogram weights.

He prioritizes compound movements and weight resistance — critical for older men to maintain muscle mass, bone density, and natural testosterone levels.

Daily Routine

Non-negotiable movement

🚴 Exercise Bike

Cardio foundation. Builds endurance, maintains cardiovascular health, and keeps the heart strong without joint impact.

🏋 Barbell Training

Compound lifts for full-body strength. Maintains bone density and functional power that deteriorates with age if untrained.

🏹 9kg Weights

Moderate weight resistance for sustained reps — preserving lean muscle mass and metabolic rate even in limited space.

💪 Compound Movements

Multi-joint exercises that recruit multiple muscle groups — maximizing efficiency and functional strength in minimal time.

Weekly Schedule

A balanced week

Consistency over intensity. This is how you train every day without burning out.

Monday
Barbell compounds + Bike 20min
Tuesday
9kg weights (high reps) + Bike 30min
Wednesday
Barbell compounds + Bodyweight
Thursday
9kg weights (high reps) + Bike 20min
Friday
Full body — Barbell + Bike 30min
Saturday
Active recovery — Bike 20min + Stretch
Sunday
Light movement — Walk or Bike 15min
Why It Works

Benefits at every level

This routine isn't random — each element targets a specific system.

🤝

Muscle Mass

Weight resistance signals your body to preserve muscle. Without it, you lose 3-5% per decade after 30.

🧠

Bone Density

Compound loading stresses bones, triggering calcification. This prevents osteoporosis and fractures.

💪

Testosterone

Heavy compound movements are the most effective natural stimulus for testosterone production at any age.

💓

Heart Health

Daily bike work keeps cardiovascular output high, resting heart rate low, and blood pressure in check.

"The harder you work for something, the greater you'll feel when you achieve it."
